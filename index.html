Darwin’s Five Claims

Claim 1: Nonconstancy of Species

Question: How do species change?

Evidence: Antibiotic resistance and ongoing bacterial evolution.

Claim 2: Descent From Common Ancestor

Evidence: Fossil records, structural DNA matches, and homologous structures.

Claim 3: Gradual Evolution

Evidence: Adaptive variations in Darwin’s finches, historical peppered moths, and dog breeding.

Claim 4: Multiplication of Species

Evidence: Speciation events driven by island isolation and adaptive radiations in Cichlid fish.

Claim 5: Natural Selection

Evidence: Modern climate change adaptations, urban wildlife behaviors, and shifting species ranges.

Evolutionary Medicine: Diseases That Changed Our DNA

Black Death

  • Agent: Yersinia pestis
  • Impact: Massive pandemic effects and ancient DNA studies point to ERAP2 gene selection.

Connection: Intense disease pressure actively altered human populations.

Sickle Cell Disease

  • Agent: HBB mutation
  • Impact: Sickle cell trait offers key malaria protection despite genetic health risks.
